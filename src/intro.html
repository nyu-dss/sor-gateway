Introduction

In 1975, Dr. Roscoe C. Brown Jr. interviewed members of the National Park Service and the Gateway National Recreation Area planning team about the opening of the site. The two Soul of Reason radio episodes that resulted cover everything from the goals guiding Gateway's development, to the natural resources of the parklands, to the nitty-gritty logistics of administering a 26,000 acre recreation site for the residents of New York, New Jersey, and surrounding states. 

These interviews yield valuable insights about the mindset and goals that guided the planning team's initial outreach efforts and shaped the management plan of the park. In addition, they also contain fascinating references to overlapping historical factors that influenced the Gateway of the 1970s and the recreation area that urban residents might know today. 

This website seeks to make these Soul of Reason episodes accessible and searchable. In the "Episodes" tab in the menu at the top of the page, users can access the digitized versions of these episodes, accompanied by time-synced transcripts and indexes. Embedded in the indexes are a number of short explanatory articles which provide context and highlight particularly fascinating portions of the interviews. Users can also access these articles by clicking the "Articles" tab in the menu.
