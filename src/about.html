About

About "Raising the Volume!” 

"Raising the Volume" is a cross-departmental NYU initiative to transform Soul of Reason, a historically rich 1970s NYC radio program on Black and Latinx culture, into a collaboratively curated collection dataset. 

The Records of NYU’s Institute for Afro-American (now African American) Affairs (IAAA), housed in NYU’s University Archives, contain a series of well over a hundred recordings of Soul of Reason, a half-hour radio show that aired from 1971-1986 on both the commercial radio station WNBC and the University’s radio station, WNYU.

Sparked by Tanisha Jones’ 2003 NYU Moving Image and Preservation (MIAP) Masters thesis, Getting to the soul of Soul of Reason: A Case for Preservation, NYU Libraries has worked to digitize and upload these recordings to their preservation repository. Copies of these audio files can be accessed via digital finding aid.

About Soul of Reason

Soul of Reason showcased unique and vital conversations with leaders, politicians, writers, activists, and artists of African descent in New York City during the civil rights and Black Power and Arts movements that spanned nearly two decades. The program’s mission, according to its founding documents, was to be “a showcase for Black scholarship… to examine the foundations of African-American thought from a variety of areas.”

Soul of Reason covered theater, film, music, art, business, literature, publishing, sports, academia, community programs, social programs, law, dentistry, mental health, and politics, with a focus on African American, Puerto Rican, and African communities. Additionally, participants on the show discuss organizations such as the Harlem Writers Guild, Black Theater Alliance, La MaMa Experimental Theatre Club, and the Nuyorican Poets Cafe; social initiatives including the Congress on Racial Equality, or CORE; and seminal works of literature and theater.

About this Site

This website seeks to make Soul of Reason's Gateway episodes accessible and searchable through digitized audio files, time-synced transcripts, and indexes, accompanied by short explanatory articles to provide context and highlight particularly fascinating portions of the interviews. 

This project was produced by Jubilee Marshall, under the guidance of Janet Bunde and the "Raising the Volume!” project team, and funded thanks to a Digital Humanities Seed Grant from the NYU Center for the Humanities. Marshall is a Master’s student in Archives and Public History at New York University.
